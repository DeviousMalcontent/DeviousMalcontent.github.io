Hexadecimal Numbering System

Hexadecimal is a positional numeral system with a base of 16. It uses sixteen distinct symbols, most often the symbols 0-9 and A, B, C, D, E, F to represent values ten to fifteen. In computing, hexadecimal numerals are usually written with a prefix, "0x".

To convert Binary to Hexadecimal we divide the binary value into 4 bit chunks, starting with the right, and padding the remaining chunk with zeros if it falls short of 4 bits.

Then we convert each individual 4 bit chunk in to its decimal value, if a decimal value is between 10 or 15 it is represented with letters A through to F respectively. As the table below illustrates;

0010 2 2
0011 3 3
0100 4 4
0101 5 5
0110 6 6
0111 7 7

1011 11 B
1100 12 C
